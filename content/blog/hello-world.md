I've been meaning to build a personal website for a while. Not a portfolio — those feel too transactional, like a resume that learned HTML. Something more like a home on the internet: a place that reflects how I actually think, not just what I've done.

## Why a blog?

I write to figure out what I think. There's a particular kind of clarity that comes from having to explain something in words — you discover gaps in your understanding that felt invisible before. If writing something down and publishing it forces me to actually understand it, that seems like a good deal.

I also like reading blogs. Not polished essays with carefully crafted arguments, but the messier kind — notes from someone working through a problem, observations they couldn't quite fit anywhere else. I hope to write more of those.

## What you'll find here

Probably a mix of:

- Notes on things I'm learning (math, biology, machine learning, programming)
- Thoughts on papers I've read
- Occasional longer pieces on ideas I find interesting
- Meta-posts like this one, which I'll try to keep to a minimum

I'll aim for honest over polished. Some posts will be rough. That's fine.

## On this site

Built with Next.js and Tailwind. The design is intentionally simple — I wanted something that didn't get in the way of reading. I'll probably keep tinkering with it.

If something here resonates, or you think I'm wrong about something (likely), I'd love to hear from you.
